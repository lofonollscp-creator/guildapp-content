# Hortoneda: chronology and historical context

## Medieval origins (958–17th century)
Hortoneda is first documented in 958 in texts from the monastery of Lavaix. It grew as a fortified village around a circular watch tower. By the 11th century the castle was already an established structure (today a National Cultural Heritage Site). In 1066 the monastery of Sant Serni de Tavèrnoles held property in the area. During the Early Modern period the territory belonged to the Marquisate of Pallars until 1831.

## Modern municipal organisation (1812–1969)
With the Cadiz Constitution (1812) an independent municipality was created. In 1847, a law requiring at least 30 residents forced the merger of Herba-savina, Hortoneda and Pessonada into the municipality of Hortoneda de la Conca, with Hortoneda as its capital. In 1969 it joined the former terms of Aramunt, Claverol and Toralla i Serradell to form the new municipality of Pallars Jussà. In 1995 this was renamed Conca de Dalt to avoid confusion with the comarca of the same name.

## The Civil War: the Pallars front (1936–1939)
During the Spanish Civil War, the Pallars front physically split the village of Hortoneda into two zones. The houses in the Segalars neighbourhood fell under Republican control; the rest remained in Francoist territory. The village had to be evacuated. Of the forty young men who took up arms, not one died in the conflict.

## The black market and post-war years (1939–1960)
Franco's autarky caused severe shortages of basic goods. Rationing cards, issued until 1952, were wholly insufficient. For mountain families, the black market (estraperlo) was the only way to obtain up to 80% of what they needed. Black-market prices were three or four times the official rates. Goods seized in Pyrenean areas included olive oil, flour, sugar, coffee and tobacco. Survival smuggling was carried out by shepherds and farmers across the Pallars.

## Smuggling in the Pallars (18th–20th century)
Pallars Jussà served as a transit and redistribution zone. Goods — tobacco, coffee, sugar, cloth, livestock — came from the north via Andorra and France, crossing mountain passes such as the Coll de Cabús (2,300 m) along the Alins–Tor–Andorra route. From La Pobla de Segur and Tremp they were distributed towards Lleida and Barcelona. The golden age of smuggling ran from roughly 1940 to 1980. Arrests dropped dramatically: 55 (2015), 5 (2017), 2 (2019), 0 (2020).

## Depopulation and present day
The population peaked in 1860 with 711 inhabitants across the former municipal area. Today Hortoneda has 22 residents and Conca de Dalt as a whole has 439 (Idescat 2025 data). The village is the main gateway to the Boumort National Game Reserve (13,097 ha, highest peak 2,077 m), the only place in Catalonia where the black vulture breeds, and one of very few spots in Europe where all four vulture species reproduce: griffon vulture, black vulture, bearded vulture and Egyptian vulture.

## Heritage of the area
- Hortoneda Castle / Torre de Roca Santa (BCIN, 11th c.): remains of a circular tower at the highest point of the village.
- Church of Santa Maria d'Hortoneda (BCIL, Romanesque, 11th c.).
- Torre de Perauba and Torre de Senyús: medieval towers within the former term.
- The Enchanted Women of Hortoneda (Les Encantades): local legends collected by writer Pep Coll.
- Roc de Santa: striking geological formation (conglomerates) and fossil mammal site.
- Barranc de l'Infern: geozone of geological interest listed by the Generalitat de Catalunya.
