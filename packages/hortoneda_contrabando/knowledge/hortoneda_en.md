# Hortoneda de la Conca: Historical, Heritage and Territorial Overview

## 1. Executive Summary

Hortoneda — historically also known as Hortoneda de la Conca or Ortoneda — is a small high-mountain village at 993 m above sea level, in the current municipality of Conca de Dalt (Pallars Jussà, Lleida, Catalonia, Spain). The village is documented since 958 and grew up around a medieval castle or watch tower. Its location commands views over the Noguera Pallaresa valley, the Conca de Dalt and the Boumort massif.

The former municipality of Hortoneda de la Conca included, in addition to Hortoneda itself, the settlements of Pessonada, Herba-savina and Vilanoveta in the Carreu valley. In 1969 it ceased to exist as an independent municipality when it merged with Aramunt, Claverol and Toralla i Serradell to form a new municipality initially called Pallars Jussà, later renamed Conca de Dalt (officially in 1995) to avoid confusion with the comarca of the same name.

## 3. Geography and Natural Setting

Hortoneda stands at 993 m at a commanding vantage point. From the village one can see the mid and high mountain ranges to the north (Pallars Sobirà), the Noguera Pallaresa valley and the whole Conca de Dalt. The main geographical features are:

- Serra del Boumort (east), highest peak 2,077 m
- Serra de Carreu (south-east)
- Sant Corneli massif (south)
- Noguera Pallaresa river (north-west boundary)

The village serves as the main gateway to the Boumort National Game Reserve. A forest track leaving from the village, near the Roc de Santa rock formation, gives direct access to the reserve.

## 5. The Spanish Civil War: A Unique Story

The Pallars front ran directly through Hortoneda during the Civil War (1936–1939). The Segalars neighbourhood fell on the Republican side; the rest of the village was in Francoist territory. The village had to be evacuated. According to the Conca de Dalt municipal archive, of the forty young men from Hortoneda who were called up to fight, not one was killed in combat — a remarkable fact for such a small community.

## 6. Architecture and Heritage

### Castle of Hortoneda / Torre de Roca Santa
The castle, also known as Torre de Roca Santa or Torre de Cal Músic, stands at the highest point of the village. Only minimal remains survive — the rubble infill of what was once a circular tower, approximately 2 m interior diameter, with walls of similar thickness. By typology it is dated to the mid or late 11th century. It is listed as a National Cultural Heritage Site (BCIN) in the Pallars Jussà.

### Church of Santa Maria d'Hortoneda
The parish church is listed as a Local Cultural Heritage Site (BCIL), with Romanesque origins in the 11th century. Pascual Madoz mentioned it in the mid-19th century as standing outside the village, with an adjoining cemetery.

### Torre de Perauba and Torre de Senyús
Two medieval towers within the former municipal term, documented in heritage inventories.

### Les Encantades d'Hortoneda
Local legends about enchanted women connected to caves, rocks and mountain passes, collected by the Pallars writer Pep Coll. Part of the mythological imagination common throughout the Pyrenees.

### Urban fabric
The village retains its mountain character: a reduced cluster of houses with grey and ochre stone facades, an arid rocky setting, and the small hamlet of Segalars to the south-east.

## 7. Geology and Natural Heritage

### Roc de Santa
A landmark geological formation of large conglomerate bodies, similar in appearance to Montserrat. It is included in the Generalitat's Inventory of Geological Interest Spaces under the geozone "Hortoneda – Roc de Santa – Barranc de l'Infern – Montsor". During World War II, lignite (low-quality coal) from the nearby lacustrine deposits was used at the La Pobla de Segur cement factory.

### Fossil site
The Roc de Santa mammal site, between the old path and the road to the village, contains vertebrate fossil remains that help reconstruct the fauna of the period and serve as a dating reference within the conglomerate mass.

### Minerals
Lignite, pyrite, goethite and calcite have been recorded at Hortoneda (MinerAtlas / Grup Mineralògic Català). Near Pessonada and Vilanoveta, iron mineralisation in oxidised Cretaceous limestone yields pyrite, goethite, haematite, calcite and quartz.

## 8. Boumort National Game Reserve

Hortoneda is one of the main access points to the Boumort National Game Reserve, one of the most important protected areas in the Catalan Pre-Pyrenees.

Key data:
- Total area: 13,097 hectares
- Highest point: Boumort peak (2,077 m)
- Location: western Catalan Pre-Pyrenees, between the Noguera Pallaresa and Segre rivers

### Wildlife
- Deer (most abundant), roe deer, chamois, fallow deer
- Protected birds: capercaillie, black woodpecker, Tengmalm's owl
- Large raptors: bearded vulture (quebrantahuesos), griffon vulture, golden eagle, peregrine falcon, Bonelli's eagle
- Exceptional fact: the black vulture (Aegypius monachus), extinct in Catalonia, was reintroduced at Boumort from 2007. It is now the only breeding site in Catalonia for this species and one of very few places in Europe where all four European vulture species reproduce.
- Mammals: wild boar, rabbit, European hare, fox, badger, pine marten, weasel, wildcat, mongoose

### Flora
Vegetation is representative of the western Catalan Pre-Pyrenees, enriched by borealpine species on the higher ridges and xerophytic Mediterranean elements on the lower slopes. Great diversity of plant communities.

## 9. Traditional Economy

Historically, Hortoneda was a subsistence mountain economy. Pascual Madoz's mid-19th-century description gives a vivid picture:
- Limited agriculture due to rough terrain and poor soil
- Production of barley, potatoes and wool
- Livestock: sheep, goats, cattle, mules, donkeys and pigs
- Firewood collection and sale as a supplementary income
- Very limited water from local springs

Today the local economy is linked to the municipality and comarca: rural tourism, natural heritage routes, forestry management and basic services.
